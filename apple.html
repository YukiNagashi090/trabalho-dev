What is an apple?

An apple is a round, edible fruit produced by the apple tree (Malus domestica). It is known for its crisp, white flesh and a skin that can be red, green, or yellow. Apples are widely cultivated and eaten raw, cooked in pies and sauces, or used to make cider.

How to know, when not to eat an apple? When it's not edible anymore.

An apple is not edible when it becomes soft, mushy, or has a wrinkled, bruised, or punctured surface. Other signs include a fermented or sour smell, a mealy or bland taste, or signs of mold or insect damage.
